Guided Learning Outcomes

Overview

Guided learning outcomes are specific, measurable statements that describe what students should know, understand, or be able to do after completing a learning activity or module. They provide clear guidance for both learners and instructors on the intended learning outcomes.

Guided learning outcomes serve several important purposes:

  • Clarity: They clarify the expectations for students by clearly defining what they are expected to achieve.
  • Focus: They help focus instructional efforts by identifying the key skills and knowledge that students should acquire.
  • Assessment: They provide a basis for assessing student learning and determining whether the learning objectives have been met.
  • Alignment: They ensure alignment between learning objectives, instructional activities, and assessments, creating a cohesive learning experience.

When writing guided learning outcomes, it's important to use action verbs that are specific, measurable, and observable. These verbs should describe the desired behaviors or actions that students should demonstrate.

Additionally, guided learning outcomes should be aligned with course objectives and instructional activities to ensure coherence and relevance.

Steps

  1. Identify learning objectives: Determine the specific goals and outcomes you want students to achieve.
  2. Choose action verbs: Select action verbs that describe the desired behaviors or actions students should demonstrate.
  3. Specify criteria: Define the criteria or standards that indicate successful achievement of the learning outcomes.
  4. Write clear statements: Write clear, concise statements that communicate the intended learning outcomes effectively.

Tips

  • Use action verbs that are specific, measurable, and observable.
  • Ensure alignment between learning outcomes, instructional activities, and assessments.
  • Include only essential outcomes that are achievable within the scope of the learning activity.
  • Review and revise learning outcomes regularly to reflect changes in instructional goals or student needs.
